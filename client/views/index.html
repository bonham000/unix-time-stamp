This service allows you to make a request to a server and view the corresponding natural and Unix time as output.

The example input: https://url_example.com/December 10 2015

returns this object: {"unix":"936576000","natural":"September 06, 1999"}

You may submit a Unix timestamp or natural date, according to these guidelines:

  • Your query must be a valid natural date of the format MM-DD-YYYY
  • The month may be abbreviated to the first three letters, or written in full
  • You may include or exclude spaces

Examples:

  • Valid: "Sep 04 2016"
  • Valid: "March 29 1999"
  • Valid: "Aug 01 2090"
  • Invalid: "Sep 4 2016"
  • Invalid: "Sept 4 2016"
  • Invalid: "Jan -1 1970"

Invalid queries will return "null" for both properties.
